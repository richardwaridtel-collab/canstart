# User Research Findings – CanStart

## Research Overview

**Objective:** Understand employment barriers for Canadian newcomers and hiring challenges for small employers; validate market demand for CanStart platform.

**Methodology:**
- Semi-structured in-depth interviews
- 65+ total participants (35+ job seekers, 30+ employers)
- Interview duration: 45-60 minutes each
- Geographic distribution: Toronto, Ottawa, Vancouver, Montreal
- Conducted: 2023-2024

---

## Job Seeker Insights (35+ interviews)

### Who We Interviewed

**Profile:**
- Recently arrived in Canada (3 months - 2 years)
- Hold valid Open Work Permit
- Have 3+ years professional experience in home country
- Actively seeking employment
- Diverse backgrounds: accounting, engineering, healthcare, IT, education

### Key Pain Points

#### 1. The "Canadian Experience" Barrier
**Quote:** "I have 10 years as an accountant, but companies say they need Canadian experience. How do I get Canadian experience without a job?"

**What we learned:**
- 100% of respondents mentioned this barrier
- Employers screening out résumés due to lack of Canadian work history
- Perceived as legitimate employer concern (not overtly discriminatory)
- Creates catch-22: Need Canadian experience to get Canadian job

#### 2. Limited Professional Networks
**Quote:** "I know my professional network in [home country], but nobody here. Nobody to refer me, advocate for me, or explain how things work."

**What we learned:**
- 95% mentioned limited network as significant barrier
- No one to informally refer them to jobs
- No mentors to explain Canadian workplace norms
- Professional network took years to build in home country
- Feels like starting from zero at age 30+

#### 3. Unfamiliar Resume/Interview Formats
**Quote:** "My CV is 4 pages. I was told Canadian résumés should be 1 page. Am I being judged for wrong format?"

**What we learned:**
- 80% mentioned uncertainty about presentation formats
- Resume format varies by country; unsure if theirs is "right"
- Interview style unfamiliar (Canadian directness, humor norms different)
- Language idioms create confusion
- Fear of being judged for differences they don't understand

#### 4. Language Proficiency Concerns
**Quote:** "My English is good, but I have an accent. I worry employers think I'm not capable because of my accent."

**What we learned:**
- 70% mentioned language as confidence concern (not necessarily actual barrier)
- Accent creates perceived risk (even when communication is clear)
- Writing proficiency often strong; speaking confidence varies
- Self-doubt about language abilities higher than actual skill level

#### 5. Credential Recognition Challenges
**Quote:** "My engineering degree is from [country]. Is it recognized here? Do I need to get it assessed?"

**What we learned:**
- 60% unsure about credential equivalency
- Professional credentials (engineering, teaching) require formal assessment
- Process for assessment unclear/expensive
- Creates uncertainty in job applications

#### 6. Fear of Employment Scams
**Quote:** "I was almost scammed in job interview where they wanted to charge me for 'training materials.' I don't know how to tell real opportunities from scams."

**What we learned:**
- 90% expressed concern about job scams
- Targeted by scams specifically because vulnerable (immigrant status, unfamiliar system)
- Real incident rate: ~15% reported being approached by scam
- Leads to extreme caution (miss legitimate opportunities by being too skeptical)
- Want verification that employer is real and legitimate

### What Job Seekers Need

**Ranked by Importance:**

1. **Legitimate, Verified Job Opportunities** (95% mentioned)
   - Confirmation employer is real
   - No upfront fees
   - Clear job description

2. **Employers Willing to Hire Newcomers** (90% mentioned)
   - Don't require "Canadian experience"
   - Willing to invest in training
   - Understand integration challenges

3. **Guidance on Canadian Workplace Culture** (85% mentioned)
   - How to interview in Canada
   - Workplace norms and expectations
   - Resume format and presentation

4. **Supportive Community** (75% mentioned)
   - Connect with others in similar situation
   - Share experiences and advice
   - Reduce isolation

5. **Fast Feedback** (80% mentioned)
   - Know job status quickly
   - Reduce 6-month uncertainty
   - Multiple opportunities simultaneously

### Market Validation: Job Seekers

**Key Question:** "Would a platform connecting you with Canadian employers willing to hire newcomers help you find a job faster?"

**Response:**
- **75% said: "Yes, definitely"**
- **15% said: "Maybe"**
- **10% said: "Not sure"**

**Willingness to Use:**
- **80% would actively use the platform**
- **65% would use daily**
- **70% would refer friends**

## Employer Insights (30+ interviews)

### Who We Interviewed

**Profile:**
- Small business owners (10-50 employees)
- Industries: Tech, healthcare, retail, administration, manufacturing
- Have open positions for entry-level or support roles
- Recent hiring experience
- Geographic distribution: Toronto, Ottawa, Vancouver, Montreal

### Key Pain Points

#### 1. Slow Hiring Process
**Quote:** "From posting to hire, it takes 2-3 months. We can't wait that long; we need people NOW."

**What we learned:**
- Average time-to-hire: 60-90 days
- Recruiting agency takes 30% commission
- Multiple rounds of interviews (3-5 interviews per candidate)
- Time cost: 20+ hours of management time
- Business impact: Open positions cost money and productivity

#### 2. High Recruitment Costs
**Quote:** "A recruiting agency charges $3,000-5,000 per hire. For entry-level positions at $40K/year, that's a huge expense."

**What we learned:**
- Agency commission: 25-30% of first year salary
- For entry-level hires: $2,000-4,000 per placement
- Direct posting: Hundreds of unqualified applications
- Manual screening: Takes 40+ hours
- Want alternatives to traditional recruitment

#### 3. Difficulty Assessing Foreign Credentials
**Quote:** "How do I know if a degree from [country] is equivalent to Canadian degree? It's a risk."

**What we learned:**
- Unfamiliar with credential equivalency assessments
- Worried about hiring unqualified candidates
- Don't trust CV claims about foreign education
- Would prefer candidates with clear Canadian credentials
- But willing to hire if credentials were verified

#### 4. Uncertainty About Cultural Fit
**Quote:** "Will they understand our workplace culture? Can they work in a diverse team? Will they need extra support?"

**What we learned:**
- Genuine concern about integration, not discrimination
- Unsure about onboarding support needed
- Want confident, self-sufficient hires
- Don't understand newcomer challenges
- Assume newcomers will need hand-holding

#### 5. Limited Access to Entry-Level Pipeline
**Quote:** "Where do I find entry-level candidates? All my networks are experienced people."

**What we learned:**
- Existing networks don't yield entry-level candidates
- LinkedIn and Indeed flooded with overqualified or unqualified
- No connection to immigrant communities
- Want access to motivated, underutilized talent
- Interested in diversity but don't know how to access it

### What Employers Need

**Ranked by Importance:**

1. **Fast Access to Pre-Qualified Candidates** (95% mentioned)
   - Candidates who meet job requirements
   - Verified work eligibility
   - Clear qualifications

2. **Simplified Hiring Process** (90% mentioned)
   - Don't want to use recruiters
   - Reduce time-to-hire
   - Direct access to candidates

3. **Verified Credentials** (85% mentioned)
   - Confirmation of education/work permits
   - Reduced risk hiring
   - Trust in application information

4. **Cultural Fit Information** (70% mentioned)
   - Understand candidate motivation and fit
   - Communication style and language
   - Willingness to learn

5. **Reduced Cost** (75% mentioned)
   - No 25-30% recruitment commission
   - Smaller upfront investment
   - Better ROI on hiring

### Market Validation: Employers

**Key Question:** "Would a platform of pre-vetted job seekers wanting entry-level positions help you hire faster and cheaper?"

**Response:**
- **80% said: "Yes, definitely"**
- **12% said: "Maybe"**
- **8% said: "Not sure"**

**Willingness to Use:**
- **75% would actively use the platform**
- **68% would post jobs regularly**
- **60% would pay for premium features**

## Cross-Segment Insights

### The "Perfect Match" Problem

**Job Seekers Want:**
- No "Canadian experience" requirement
- Employers willing to train
- Verified, legitimate employers

**Employers Want:**
- Candidates without Canadian experience (cheaper, trainable)
- Motivated, hungry employees
- Reduced hiring process

**Insight:** Perfect alignment. Newcomers' "weakness" (no Canadian experience) is actually employer's preference (cheaper, more trainable). Marketplace solves for both.

### Trust is the Core Issue

**For Job Seekers:**
- Fear of scams prevents job searching
- Want verification that employer is real
- Willing to share personal info if platform is trusted

**For Employers:**
- Fear of hiring unqualified candidates
- Want verification of credentials
- Concerned about integration challenges

**Insight:** Trust system (verification + ratings) is THE critical feature. Everything else is secondary.

### Both Groups Value Speed

**Job Seekers:** Want to know job status in days, not weeks
**Employers:** Want to hire in weeks, not months

**Insight:** Fast matching algorithm and clear communication are high-impact features.

---

## Key Insights Summary

1. **Problem is REAL**
   - Both groups independently identified same barriers
   - Not prompted; spontaneously mentioned pain points
   - High frustration levels (8/10 average)

2. **Solution Resonates**
   - 75-80% interest rate is strong validation
   - Both groups see clear personal benefit
   - Willing to adopt new platform if solves problem

3. **Market is Ready**
   - Newcomer numbers increasing annually
   - Employer hiring pressure acute
   - Technology adoption normalized
   - Window of opportunity is open

4. **Trust is Critical**
   - Without verification, platform fails
   - Job seekers won't use unvetted platform
   - Employers won't hire without credential confidence
   - Ratings system essential for ongoing trust

5. **Network Effects Are Strong**
   - More job seekers = more attractive to employers
   - More employers = more attractive to job seekers
   - First platform to reach critical mass wins

## Next Steps

**Based on research findings:**

1. **Build MVP with Trust System First**
   - Verification (work permit, business legitimacy)
   - Profiles that build confidence
   - Clear communication channels

2. **Focus on User Experience**
   - Both groups need simplicity
   - Clear job descriptions (no ambiguity)
   - Fast feedback loops

3. **Build Community Features Early**
   - Ratings and reviews
   - Success stories
   - Peer support

4. **Launch with Settlement Agency Partners**
   - They already serve job seeker market
   - Can drive initial users
   - Trust transfer from agency to platform

5. **Validate Willingness to Pay**
   - Employers said 60% would pay for premium
   - Test pricing with MVP
   - Determine sustainable revenue model

---

**Research Conclusion:**

CanStart solves a **real, validated market problem** with **strong demand signals** from both user groups. Market is ready for disruption. Platform has clear path to product-market fit.
